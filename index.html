What is Fixed Struggle?

Fixed Struggle represents a situation where you experience constant struggle. Its an almost constant struggle living in poverty and trying to improve your situation. Currently, 1 billion children worldwide are living in poverty. The odds that a child who is born into poverty of having the same future status is high. Education is the best hope we have to break the cycle of multi-generational poverty. Fixed Struggle was created to spread awareness and create a conversation about giving each kid the opportunity and the resources to achieve greatness.

The Cause

I've started an education fundraiser with Mayan Families. I was introduced to their cause through the Living on One Dollar documentary. Their multi-dimensional approach to fighting poverty in Guatamala has helped improve the lives for thousands of Guatemalan families.

The Challenge

Bike as many hills and peaks on my fixed gear bicycle as possible to see how many feet of elevation gain I can climb.

To achieve max elevation, I will be traveling from Dayton, OH and spending the month in Colorado. My first goal is to reach the height of Mt. Fuji. Standing at 12,389 ft, Mt. Fuji is the tallest mountain in Japan. Due to the sheer quantity of elevation in Colorado, twelve-thousand feet should be achievable within my first handful of rides. If I make it to the top of Mt. Fuji, I'll set a new milestone and so on.

The Struggle

I'm not expecting this to be easy. The plan is to spend the month of September, climbing and reclimbing hills in Dayton, OH. Each week, I will increase my target elevation gain by a thousand. This method helps my legs get use to the climbing, but I have no way of training for how constant and continuous some of the climbs in Colorado are. The other big thing that, being from Ohio, dont have much experience with, are the side effects of hanging out above 8,000ft above sea level aka altitude sickness.

I'll be posting my goal for each training ride on my facebook page and invite anyone to conquer and share with me.
